About

I'm an undergraduate at UNC Chapel Hill studying Biology and Computer Science — working at the interface where molecular simulation meets statistical learning.

My research starts from a simple premise: static structures don't tell the full story. A protein's function emerges from the ensemble of conformations it visits over time. I use enhanced-sampling methods like MetaDynamics and Gaussian accelerated MD to reconstruct the energy landscapes that govern these motions, then build ML models that compress thousands of trajectories into testable predictions.

Right now I'm splitting my time across three labs — running free energy simulations at Caltech, training kinetics predictors at UNC, and building the HPC infrastructure that makes it all reproducible at scale.

"Simulations produce data. Learning compresses it. The model tells you which experiment to run next."

Caltech · Anima AI+Science Lab

SURF Research Fellow · $8,200 fellowship

GenSLM-230 and natural NdTrpB have a backbone RMSD of just 0.36 Å — structurally indistinguishable. Yet one is catalytically active and the other is not. I'm using well-tempered MetaDynamics with path collective variables to reconstruct the COMM domain free energy surface across the Open → Partially Closed → Closed transition, asking whether conformational dynamics can explain what static structure prediction misses. Running 10-walker campaigns at 350K with PLUMED2+GROMACS on UNC Longleaf.

UNC · Miao Lab

Research Assistant

Can you predict how long a drug stays bound from simulation alone? Using LiGaMD3 enhanced sampling across ~130 protein-ligand systems (HSP90, CDK2, HIV protease), I've engineered 120,000+ trajectory-derived features — energy gradients, contact frequencies, binding-site volume fluctuations — into regression models that predict dissociation rates (koff) where static descriptors fail. Currently expanding from 35 completed systems toward the full dataset.
