About

I am an undergraduate at UNC Chapel Hill pursuing dual degrees in Biology and Computer Science.

Most of my time goes to a question that sits between physics and learning: when a protein samples thousands of conformations per microsecond, which motions actually determine whether a drug binds, how fast it arrives, and how long it stays?

The through-line is straightforward: simulations produce data, learning compresses it into a model, and the model tells you which experiment to run next.
