UNC Miao Lab

Machine learning + molecular dynamics for protein-ligand binding kinetics

Jun 2025 – Present PI: Prof. Yinglong Miao UNC Chapel Hill Focus: LiGaMD3 Enhanced Sampling

Research Context

Predicting how tightly and how quickly a drug binds its target is central to drug design. While binding affinity (ΔG) is routinely computed, binding kinetics, the on-rate (kon) and off-rate (koff), remain extremely challenging. The Miao Lab developed LiGaMD3 (Ligand Gaussian accelerated Molecular Dynamics 3), an enhanced sampling method that can capture complete ligand binding and unbinding events within computationally accessible timescales.

My Work

Data Engineering Pipeline

Built a Python pipeline (pandas/NumPy) curating ~130 protein-ligand systems from KIND13 and PDBbind databases. Transformed 120,000+ MD time-series records into structured features including energy gradients, motion vectors, contact frequencies, and geometric descriptors. The pipeline handles multiple target systems:

  • HSP90 (Heat Shock Protein 90): Important cancer drug target, 8 ligand systems
  • CDK2 (Cyclin-Dependent Kinase 2): Cell cycle regulation, 12 ligand systems
  • HIV Protease: Antiretroviral target, multiple inhibitor complexes

Feature Engineering from Dynamics

Leveraged LiGaMD3 trajectories to capture hidden intermediates and transition states that are invisible in static crystal structures. Applied PCA + K-Means clustering to identify representative low-energy conformational ensembles. Key features extracted:

  • Boost potentials and reweighting factors from GaMD
  • Residue-ligand contact frequencies over time
  • Binding site volume fluctuations
  • Interaction fingerprint time-series (hydrogen bonds, hydrophobic contacts, salt bridges)

Predictive Modeling

Developed ML models (scikit-learn + PyTorch) fusing 3D structural descriptors with trajectory-derived transition features. Validated improved prediction of kon/koff signals versus static-only baselines.

Technical Challenges

  • Convergence assessment: How to know when LiGaMD3 simulations have sampled enough binding/unbinding events
  • Feature selection: Balancing trajectory-derived features (rich but noisy) with structural features (sparse but clean)
  • System diversity: Models trained on HSP90 need to generalize to CDK2 and HIV protease
