About Me

I'm a computational biology researcher at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, pursuing dual degrees in Biology (B.S.) and Computer Science (B.S.). My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and molecular science, where I develop and apply enhanced-sampling molecular dynamics methods to study protein-ligand interactions.

In the Miao Lab, I run Gaussian accelerated Molecular Dynamics (GaMD) and Ligand GaMD (LiGaMD3) simulations targeting therapeutically relevant systems including HSP90, CDK2, and HIV-related proteins. My research pipeline spans system preparation, production simulations on UNC's Longleaf HPC cluster, and free energy analysis.

Beyond molecular dynamics, I build full-stack applications and data science tools. I'm passionate about bringing software engineering rigor to scientific computing and making computational methods more accessible and reproducible.
