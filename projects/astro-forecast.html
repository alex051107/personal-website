Astro Forecasting MLOps Platform

LSTM-based exoplanet discovery trend forecasting with 7-dimensional habitability scoring

Duration May – Sep 2025
Team 4-member team lead
Event UNC 48-hour Data Science Hackathon
Data 50,000+ NASA observations

Overview

Led a 4-member team in UNC's 48-hour Data Science Hackathon. The challenge: analyze 30+ years of NASA exoplanet observations to forecast discovery trends and inform telescope resource allocation.

Data Pipeline

Built a reproducible data and feature pipeline (pandas/NumPy) reconciling inconsistent observations across 30+ years of data:

  • Standardized discovery methods (radial velocity, transit, imaging, microlensing)
  • Handled missing values in planetary parameters (mass, radius, orbital period, equilibrium temperature)
  • Feature engineering: discovery rate by method, cumulative counts, seasonal patterns, technology capability indicators

Forecasting Models

Compared multiple approaches:

  • ARIMA/SARIMA: Traditional time-series baseline
  • Holt-Winters: Triple exponential smoothing for trend + seasonality
  • LSTM (PyTorch): Primary model, capturing non-linear patterns in discovery rates

Result: 18% lower RMSE vs. baselines, MAPE within ±7%

7-Dimensional Habitability Scoring

Developed a composite habitability score incorporating:

  1. Equilibrium temperature (proximity to 255K Earth reference)
  2. Stellar flux ratio
  3. Planet radius (Earth-like range: 0.8-1.6 R⊕)
  4. Orbital eccentricity (low = more stable climate)
  5. Host star metallicity
  6. Stellar type classification
  7. Orbital period (habitable zone proxy)

Dynamic scoring handles incomplete multi-metric inputs by re-normalizing available dimensions.

Validation

Validated forecasts against newly observed discoveries with ~70% match rate across multiple prediction horizons.

Interactive Dashboard

Built with Plotly Dash featuring:

  • Trend visualization across discovery methods
  • Residual diagnostics for model performance
  • Feature-importance heatmaps
  • Habitability score explorer with drill-down by planetary system
